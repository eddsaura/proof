## Design Context

### Users
SOTI House is for builders from around the world who met through the House and want to keep the connection alive beyond the web. They use the app to share what they are learning, keep async rhythm through threaded discussion, and discover who they can meet when they arrive in a city.

### Brand Personality
High-energy, intimate, and sharp. The interface should feel like a warm creative network for serious builders: focused enough for repeated daily use, human enough to make people want to return, and distinct enough to feel like SOTI rather than a generic community app.

### Aesthetic Direction
Use a dark, high-contrast design as the primary expression: dark grey foundations, almost-white text, warm cream links and mentions, and minimal ornamental noise. Primary actions should feel white, clear, and decisive rather than accent-colored. The light theme should feel like an inversion of that language rather than a separate beige product: pale neutral surfaces, near-black text, and the same restrained accent logic.

### Cross-Platform Direction
The product should be easy to read and contribute to from both phone and desktop. Favor spacious, lightly editorial composition with clean product UX: readable line lengths, calm density, generous vertical rhythm, and content-width constraints on desktop so feeds, threads, and forms do not stretch too wide.

### Design Principles
- Never use cards for posts, timeline items, or comments. They waste space and break the calm reading rhythm. Use open layouts, dividers, grouped rows, pinned actions, full-width bands, and typographic hierarchy before reaching for containers.
- Reply composers should also be open sections, not framed cards. The input itself can be a control, but the whole reply area should not sit inside a rounded container.
- Cards are only acceptable for focused tools, modals, and genuinely framed states. Do not nest cards inside cards.
- Do not add small all-caps eyebrow labels above headings such as "SOTI HOUSE", "CREATE", or "INBOX". Let the main heading and navigation carry context.
- The product should feel useful, alive, and spacious, not like a marketing page. The first screen should prioritize the community feed and action paths.
- High contrast is part of the brand. Text should be almost white on dark grey, or near black on pale neutral surfaces in light mode.
- Accents should be rare and meaningful: mentions, links, reply actions, notifications, and map/member signals. Navigation, selected controls, and primary commands should use white/ink states rather than the cream accent.

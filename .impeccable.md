## Design Context

### Users
SOTI House is for builders from around the world who met through the House and want to keep the connection alive beyond the web. They use the app to share what they are learning, keep async rhythm through threaded discussion, and discover who they can meet when they arrive in a city.

### Brand Personality
High-energy, intimate, and sharp. The interface should feel like a private room for serious builders: focused enough for repeated daily use, warm enough to make people want to return, and distinct enough to feel like SOTI rather than a generic community app.

### Aesthetic Direction
Use a dark, high-contrast design as the primary expression: dark grey foundations, almost-white text, rare sharp accents, and minimal ornamental noise. The light theme should feel like an inversion of that language rather than a separate beige product: pale neutral surfaces, near-black text, and the same restrained accent logic.

### Design Principles
- Do not design every object as a card. Use open layouts, dividers, grouped rows, pinned actions, full-width bands, and typographic hierarchy before reaching for containers.
- Cards are reserved for repeated content items, focused tools, and genuinely framed states. Do not nest cards inside cards.
- Do not add small all-caps eyebrow labels above headings such as "SOTI HOUSE", "CREATE", or "INBOX". Let the main heading and navigation carry context.
- The product should feel dense, useful, and alive, not like a marketing page. The first screen should prioritize the community feed and action paths.
- High contrast is part of the brand. Text should be almost white on dark grey, or near black on pale neutral surfaces in light mode.
- Accents should be rare and meaningful: active category, primary command, mention, notification, or map/member signal.
